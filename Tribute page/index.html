Dr. Vikram Sarabhai

1919 - 1979

Vikram Sarabhai was a visionary scientist who played a pivotal role in establishing India's space program.

Dr. Vikram Sarabhai (1919 - 1979) was a visionary scientist and innovator who played a crucial role in shaping India's modern identity. As the founder of India's space program, he pioneered the use of space technology for national development, paving the way for India to become a major player in the global space industry. His relentless pursuit of excellence and dedication to his work continue to inspire generations of scientists, entrepreneurs, and leaders in India and beyond.

The Legacy of Vikram Sarabhai

Vikram Sarabhai's legacy lives on today in the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), which he founded in 1969. His contributions to the field of space research were immense, and he played a key role in shaping India's space program. He was a firm believer in using technology for the betterment of society, and his work in developing India's first satellite, Aryabhata, paved the way for many more successes to come. Today, ISRO is one of the world's leading space agencies, and continues to inspire generations of scientists and engineers to push the boundaries of space exploration. Vikram Sarabhai's vision and passion for science and technology have left an indelible mark on India's scientific landscape, and his legacy continues to inspire and influence countless individuals around the world.

Biographies

  • Name: Vikram Sarabhai
  • Place of Birth: Ahmedabad, India
  • Father’s Name: Ambalal Sarabhai
  • Mother’s Name: Saraladevi Sarabhai
  • Education:
  • 1/ Gujarat College, Ahmedabad
  • 2/ University of Cambridge, England (tripos in natural sciences) in 1940.
  • 3/ University of Cambridge, England to pursue his PhD and wrote a thesis, "Cosmic Ray Investigations in Tropical Latitudes", in 1947.
  • positions:
  • 1/ President of the Physics section, Indian Science Congress (1962)
  • 2/ President of the General Conference of the I.A.E.A., Vienna (1970)
  • 3/ Chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission of India (1966–1971)
  • 4/ Vice-president, Fourth UN Conference on 'Peaceful uses of Atomic Energy' (1971)
  • 5/ Founder and Chairman (1963–1971), Space Applications Centre
  • Death: 30 December 1971
